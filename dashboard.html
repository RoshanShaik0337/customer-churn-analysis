Key Findings — Real IBM Telco Data

Month-to-month customers churn at 42.7% — 16.1 points above the 26.6% average. Contract type is the single strongest predictor of churn.

Electronic check payers churn at 45.3% — the highest of all payment methods, suggesting payment friction or lower commitment levels.

Fiber optic users churn at 41.9% despite paying the highest charges — a clear signal of service quality or pricing dissatisfaction.

New customers (0–6 months) churn at 53.3% — more than half leave within their first 6 months, identifying a critical onboarding failure.

Loyal customers (49+ months) churn at just 9.5% — reaching the 4-year mark is a strong indicator of long-term retention.

Business Recommendations

R1 — Contract Conversion Campaign Offer 15–20% discount to Month-to-month customers upgrading to annual contracts. If 30% convert, estimated churn reduction of 18–22%.
R2 — Fiber Optic Quality Review Deploy NPS survey exclusively to Fiber optic users. Fast-track top complaints with a 60-day SLA improvement target to address 41.9% churn.
R3 — Auto-Pay Incentive Offer $5/month discount for switching from Electronic check to bank transfer or credit card auto-pay — targets the 45.3% churn risk segment.
R4 — 90-Day Onboarding Program Proactive check-ins at day 7, 30, and 90 for new customers. Targeting the 53.3% early churn rate with structured onboarding support.
